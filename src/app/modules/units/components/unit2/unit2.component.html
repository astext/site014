The Hill/Sallis collaborations

Sometime in Sydney

Stephen and I decided to do music together in June 1978 during a conversation on my birthday at the Rex Hotel, Sydney. We started writing several songs, intended as SPK material. Regardless of the vexed and unreliable narrative of SPK as it is officially fetishised and jealously possessed by later stakeholders, Stephen and I wrote and rehearsed hard and often, months before I eventually auditioned for Graeme Revell in late 1978/early 1979(?) to perform with his version of the band. No More was the first song we wrote together and that first set of emerging songs included prototypes of Stephen's early lyrics for SPK OK and Contact later used in the 'industrial' format they perceived as more suitable to the times. Our other unreconstructed punk songs were Shortfuse, Mommy Daddy and Me, Hiroshima/Nagasaki (Holocaust Me) and others. We played our first gig at the Tin Sheds annex, Sydney University, in 1978. Later I played bass with Stephen, Graeme and Danny Rumour a) at the Garibaldi's gig, and b) on the Stephen Jones video. No More appears on the first SPK single.
